# How to Present This Site to Management

## Recommended flow
1. **Overview** — explain the big shift: from tutorial maintenance to clearer ownership.
2. **Strategic Objective** — explain why ownership matters beyond formatting.
3. **Detailed Q1 Workstreams** — show what was done and why it matters.
4. **Proof of Work** — open the files only when your boss wants proof.
5. **Tutorial Improvement Gallery** — use this to make the work visible quickly.
6. **Category Progress Dashboard** — explain how Q1 was organized by category, not random edits.
7. **Systems & Tools** — show that this work is supported by strategy, documentation, and automation.
8. **Deep Dive: Early Business Signals** — be careful here; frame everything as early assisted-commerce signals, not direct revenue proof.
9. **Q2 Plan** — end on activation, measurement, and reporting.

## Best narrative
- Q4 2025: I built the direction.
- Q1 2026: I took ownership and built the foundation.
- Q2 2026: I want to make the value easier to measure and report.

## What not to overclaim
- Do not say tutorials directly drove sales.
- Say tutorials are already showing early search, add-to-cart, and order-linked signals.
- Be clear that tracking is still incomplete and Q2 is about improving that.

## What to emphasize
- Standardization system
- Category-based restructuring
- Operations ownership
- Telegram automation takeover and extension
- Supporting tools built around the platform
- Clearer business measurement direction
